Programming in P5 - is not a normal programming book

By Henrik Sterner and Peter Sterner

Tired of programming books that feel like punishment for a crime you didn’t commit? Programming in P5 is here to rescue you from the land of grey code and drop you into a world of colour, movement, and glorious chaos. This isn’t about writing thousands of lines of code just to make a calculator that can add 2 + 2 (spoiler: it’s 4). Instead, you’ll learn programming by creating things that actually do something fun — bouncing shapes, interactive doodles, and visuals that dance around like they’ve had too much coffee. It’s perfect for artists, designers, or anyone who secretly suspects computers could be more than spreadsheets and tax returns. With Programming in P5, coding becomes less “serious business” and more “look what I just made — isn’t it cool?!” So grab your imagination, bring your sense of humour, and prepare to explore the wonderfully weird intersection where art and technology collide… with plenty of sparks.
